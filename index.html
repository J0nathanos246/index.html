Uses of Electric and Magnetic Feilds within Particle Accelerators

Abstract:

Linacs and cyclotrons are the two particle accelerators researched for this article.
Linacs accelerate particles in a straight line and uses oppositely charged drift tubes
connected to a pulse waveform to accelerate particles. Cyclotrons use a magnet split
into two dees oppositely charged to deflect the charged particles to follow a circular
path. With both of these examples electric and magnetic fields are in use, the particles
are only accelerated when crossing the gap between dees or drift tubes. Synchrotron
radian is used to find the particles relativistic momentum and charge, it also tells us
that any charged particle moving through a charged electric or magnetic field would
experience a force. The Lorentz’s factor is used for these particles when moving at
relativistic speeds. Theres also limitations that we face when dealing with particle
accelerators, these are the size and strength of magnets required to keep the high
energy beams on course and the size of accelerators used.

Introduction

Today in the world there are more than 35000 particle accelerators, all used in a wide varity
of different applications in physics, chemistry, medicine and structure analysis. The largest
particle accelerator (Large Hadron Collider) can be found in Geneva Switzerland, this can
create beams of energy of 7 Tev.

Types of particle accelerators:

Linear particle accelerators (linac) use accelerator modules aslo known as drift tubes, that are
cylindrical hollow electrodes, coupled behind each other connected to a pulsed waveform
that generates an accelerating gap between the two oppositely charged electrodes. For
positively charged particles, these oppositely charged particles repel the particle from
positive electrode and attracts them towards the negative electrode over the gap between
them. The gap between modules increases when particles have low energy this is due to the
particles accelerating at a greater magnitude when at low speeds, but when particles have
high energy the distance of the acceleration gap doesn’t change size.
